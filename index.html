Story reading and analysis-II


The story begins with Yan's determination to find deer. He returns to a trail he had previously explored, this time encountering numerous fresh tracks that initially confuse him.
Yan follows the freshest tracks, leading to his first sighting of two deer.
He's awestruck by their appearance and behaviour, noting how they seem to look with both their eyes and ears.
Yan observes the deer playing, bounding up and down effortlessly.
After the deer leave, Yan examines their tracks. He's amazed to discover the incredible distance between each set of tracks, realising the deer's bounds cover 18 to 30 feet at a time.
Yan concludes that the deer don't run, but essentially fly, only touching the ground occasionally. He expresses gladness that the deer escaped, recognising the unique nature of his observation.
